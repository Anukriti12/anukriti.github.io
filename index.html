About Me

Hi! I am a Research Fellow (Pre-Doctoral Researcher) at Microsoft Research, India in the Technology and Empowerment group, advised by Dr. Saikat Guha where I have been working towards democratizing the availability of printed content (specifically, scientific publications) and making them more accessible for the blind, low-vision, and other print-impaired (BLV) individuals. I am also working with Dr. Mohit Jain and Dr. Nipun Kwatra on understanding the usage and side effects of smartphone devices on the human eye. I am developing an android application which uses the smartphone camera to track various eye parameters. These can then be analyzed to provide actionable feedback to users to encourage behavior change.

Prior to this, I graduated with a B.Tech in Information Technology from Delhi Technological University (Formerly, Delhi College of Engineering) in 2021 with University Rank 2 in 2360 students. During my time there, I worked on a variety of research topics in Machine Learning and Computer Vision including biometric based human identification, person verification, text to image synthesis, information extraction systems etc. I have also interned at Samsung R&D Institute, Linkedin, DE Shaw, IIT Delhi and NTU, Singapore. I have had the good fortune to work with Dr. Chetan Arora, Dr. Siew-Kei Lam, Dr. Dinesh K. Vishwakarma and Dr. Thambipillai Srikanthan.

My research experiences have given me a unique opportunity to build, deploy and evaluate novel technologies and interactive learning systems in various domains including healthcare, education, social computing, accessibility and others. I have come to realize the importance of working on research problems that are applicable in the real world and strive to have this flavor in my research. I intend to specialize in Human-AI Interaction, with a focus on applying state-of-the-art machine learning methods to create AI-driven, human-centered interactive systems that can be beneficial to humans or can have a real-world impact.
